# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary users are gym and community participants joining a private World Cup prediction game run by their organization. For TM Boxing, users are members who already paid their June fee and receive a private signup link from the gym owners. They usually access the experience from mobile, often in short focused sessions, and need to understand the tournament flow quickly without feeling like they are using a corporate tool.

Secondary users are tenant operators and gym owners. They need a simple operational surface to share the signup link, review participant volume, disable users when needed, and load official results without depending on technical support.

## Product Purpose
Prode Empresas is a multi-tenant B2B platform for running branded World Cup prediction experiences for organizations. Each tenant gets its own branded space, private participant access, prediction flow, ranking, and operator controls.

For TM Boxing, success means three things: participants can register and enter without friction, the prediction flow feels clear and trustworthy on mobile, and the gym owners can manage access and participants without confusion.

## Brand Personality
The product should feel direct, energetic, and grounded. For TM Boxing specifically, the tone is closer to a gym community than a formal company portal: welcoming, confident, and simple under pressure.

The interface should help people feel that:
- this is private and organized
- they can complete the prode without getting lost
- the gym is in control of the experience

## Anti-references
- Generic corporate SaaS dashboards with cold blue palettes and lifeless admin tables.
- Over-decorated sports microsites that sacrifice clarity for hype.
- Overly playful onboarding that makes the bracket and scoring system harder to understand.
- Dense spreadsheet-like mobile layouts that force horizontal scanning for core tasks.

## Design Principles
- Make the prediction flow feel guided: each step should explain what matters now, what comes next, and what is still pending.
- Prioritize mobile comprehension over desktop density: if a participant cannot understand the current task quickly on a phone, the design is not finished.
- Keep operational trust visible: owners and participants should always understand whether access, ranking, and results are current and private.
- Preserve TM Boxing's community character: the product should feel branded and human, not like a generic white-label portal.
- Reduce cognitive load before adding flair: motion and polish should support orientation, not compete with the task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA level for contrast and focus states. Support reduced-motion-friendly interactions and avoid relying on color alone for status. Keep touch targets generous for mobile users, keep labels explicit for first-time participants, and make the core flow understandable without assumed technical knowledge.
